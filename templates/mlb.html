Moneyball: A 20 Year Glance

Money Spent

Investigating Cost Per Win

This scatterplot gives an overview of how effectively each of Major League Baseball’s 30 teams were in getting the most “bang for their buck” over the last 20 seasons (excluding the shortened 2020 season). With 600 data points, this is merely an overview, and shouldn’t be relied upon for in-depth analysis. The actual cost per win data, broken down by team and year, is available elsewhere on this site.
It is instructive to notice however that majority of Oakland’s teams during this 20-year period (as denoted by the Kelly Green plots), populate the upper right-hand portion of the chart. As a handy comparison, the 20 World Series winning teams from this period are indicated by pink plots on the chart. Further analysis of Oakland’s success, as compared to the league as a whole is available on the Final Analysis page.
